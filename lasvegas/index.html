FAQ

What safety precautions will Big Smoke Las Vegas be taking due to COVID-19 concerns?

UPDATE (AUGUST 2021): Proof of Vaccination or a Negative PCR or Rapid Test within 48 hours of the event is now REQUIRED to attend Big Smoke Las Vegas. We feel strongly this is in the best interest of all attendees and exhibitors. Information regarding the verification of vaccination and negative tests will be shared closer to the event.

Nevada has reintroduced a mask mandate due to the increased spread of the COVID-19 delta variant. After discussion with the Mirage Hotel and Casino, we are happy to share that a mask is not required while actively smoking, eating or drinking. We ask, however, that if you are not actively smoking, eating or drinking at the event, that you please wear a mask around the event floor. Please note the health situation is ever changing and this guidance is subject to change between now and November.

If you have already purchased a ticket and will not be fully vaccinated or able to provide proof of a negative test by the time of the event, you are welcome to request a full refund (less processing fees) until October 4th, 2021. After October 4th, 2021 there are no refunds. If you wish to request a refund, please email support@squadup.com.

 

Is Big Smoke Las Vegas shorter this year?

Yes, Big Smoke Las Vegas is two days instead of three this year. Cigar seminars will only be hosted on Saturday.

 

Can I smoke at the event?

Yes, cigars can be enjoyed in the ballrooms at the Mirage Event Center.

 

Will there be food at the Big Smoke Evenings?

Yes, there will be a delicious selection of food offered in a safe and healthy manner.

 

Can I purchase a ticket to attend a Seminar?

Seminars are included in the weekend package. Individual seminar tickets are not available.

 

Dress Code?

Be casual. Enjoy the night!

 

Additional Questions?

Send us an e-mail at events@mshanken.com. We will be happy to answer them!
